Case-Study Workbook

Eight fictional workplace case studies for hands-on practice. For each case, diagnose the weak prompt, then build a stronger one using the advanced framework. Type directly into this page or print it for handwriting. There are no trainer answers here — the goal is your own practice.

How to work through each case
Read the scenario and the fictional source, note what is wrong with the weak prompt, complete the framework worksheet, then write your improved prompt and one follow-up prompt. Case Study 1

Manufacturing shift handover

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

You are finishing a shift on a fictional packing line and must hand over to the next shift. Your notes are rough, and the next shift needs to know status, open issues and what to watch for.

Fictional source input

Line P-2 ran normally most of the shift. Around mid-shift the labeller jammed twice; cleared both times, maintenance informed. One tote of caps is waiting for a replacement label reel (reel number to be confirmed). Housekeeping done. Two operators trained on the new checklist. Restart time for the pending tote not yet known.

Weak prompt

Weak promptWrite a handover.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • No clear goal — "write a handover" does not say what the next shift must know.
  • No role or audience — outgoing operator vs next-shift team is never specified.
  • No source grounding — the fictional notes are not attached or referenced.
  • No output format — sections such as status, open issues and watch-outs are missing.
  • No constraints — nothing stops the model inventing a restart time or other facts.

Your notes:

Your task

Rewrite the prompt using the framework so the next shift gets a clear, structured handover based only on the source above.

Hint: think about role, the exact sections you want, and a constraint that stops the model inventing a restart time.

Case Study 2

QA observation summary

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

During a fictional routine walk-through, you jotted several general observations and want a neutral, structured summary to share with your team lead for discussion.

Fictional source input

Corridor lighting near store area dim in one spot. A general noticeboard had an out-of-date notice. Two labelled sample trays not returned to their marked location. A hand-sanitiser station was empty. All observations general and non-specific; no product or batch involved.

Weak prompt

Weak promptTell me how serious these QA issues are and what the root cause is.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • Asks for prohibited decisions — severity rating and root cause are not for the model.
  • Wrong goal — it seeks judgement instead of a neutral observation summary.
  • Invites speculation — the source gives only general notes, not causes.
  • No audience or output format — not framed for a team-lead discussion table.
  • No constraints — nothing keeps the output descriptive and non-conclusive.

Your notes:

Your task

Rewrite the prompt to produce a neutral, structured observation summary. The prompt must not ask the model to rate severity or conclude a cause.

Framework reminder: use constraints to keep the output descriptive only, and an output format with columns for observation, location and a follow-up question.

Case Study 3

Cross-functional meeting minutes

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

You took quick notes in a fictional cross-functional coordination meeting and need clean minutes with clear decisions and actions.

Fictional source input

Present: representatives from planning, stores and administration. Discussed a new internal request form; agreed to pilot it for two weeks. Planning to draft the form; stores to list required fields; admin to arrange a shared folder. Follow-up meeting suggested but date not fixed. One open question about who approves the final form.

Weak prompt

Weak promptMake minutes from these notes and fill in anything missing.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • "Fill in anything missing" explicitly invites hallucination.
  • No constraint to mark unknowns as "to be confirmed".
  • No minutes structure — attendees, decisions, actions and open questions are unspecified.
  • No role or audience for who will read and confirm the minutes.
  • Gaps in the notes (follow-up date, approver) would likely be invented.

Your notes:

Your task

Write a prompt that produces formal minutes with attendees, decisions, actions (owner and due date) and open questions — using only the notes, with unknowns marked clearly.

Hint: "fill in anything missing" is the flaw. Replace it with a constraint that marks unknowns as "to be confirmed".

Case Study 4

HR policy simplification

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

A fictional internal note about a new flexible-hours trial is written in dense language. You want a plain-language version for all staff, without changing the meaning.

Fictional source input

"A trial of flexible start times (between 8:00 and 10:00) will operate for the administration team for six weeks, subject to team coverage being maintained and manager confirmation. Core hours remain unchanged. Feedback will be gathered at the end of the trial."

Weak prompt

Weak promptMake this policy simple and add the benefits.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • "Add the benefits" asks for claims that are not in the source.
  • No "no new information" constraint — meaning can drift.
  • Audience is vague — "simple" is not the same as plain language for all staff.
  • No instruction to preserve every fact (times, duration, conditions).
  • No output format or quality check for faithfulness to the original note.

Your notes:

Your task

Write a prompt that produces a plain-language version for all staff, preserving every fact and not adding benefits or claims that are not in the source.

Framework reminder: audience-aware plus a strict "no new information" constraint.

Case Study 5

IT incident communication

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

A fictional shared drive was briefly unavailable this morning and is now restored. You must inform staff simply and reassuringly, without technical detail or speculation about the cause.

Fictional source input

Shared drive "TeamDocs" was unavailable from about 9:10 to 9:45 today. Access is now restored. No action needed from users. Cause under review by IT; details not yet confirmed. Contact the IT helpdesk for any lingering access issue.

Weak prompt

Weak promptWrite an IT email and explain what caused the outage.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • Asks to explain the cause when the source says it is under review and unconfirmed.
  • Invites speculation and invented technical detail.
  • No audience or tone — not framed as a short, reassuring staff notice.
  • No constraint to use only confirmed facts from the source.
  • No clear output format (what happened, status, what to do, contact).

Your notes:

Your task

Write a prompt that produces a short, reassuring notice using only the source, explicitly avoiding any unconfirmed cause.

Hint: add a constraint: "do not speculate about the cause; state it is under review".

Case Study 6

R&D technical simplification

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

A fictional internal experiment note uses technical language. You want a plain-language summary for a non-technical operations audience, keeping every fact and all uncertainty intact.

Fictional source input

"A bench trial compared two mixing times for a fictional model blend. The longer time appeared to give a more uniform mix in this small trial, but the difference was small and results varied between runs. Further testing is suggested before any conclusion."

Weak prompt

Weak promptSummarise this and tell operations which mixing time is best.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • Asks which mixing time is "best" — a technical recommendation the model must not make.
  • Flattens uncertainty — the source says the difference was small and results varied.
  • Wrong goal for a non-technical audience — seeks a decision, not a faithful summary.
  • No constraint to preserve uncertainty or avoid conclusions.
  • No plain-language or output-format guidance for operations readers.

Your notes:

Your task

Write a prompt for a plain-language summary that preserves the uncertainty and does not recommend a "best" option.

Framework reminder: constraint = "preserve uncertainty; do not state a conclusion or recommendation".

Case Study 7

L&D training-content creation

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

You are preparing a fictional 30-minute refresher on writing clear internal emails and need learning objectives plus a short session outline.

Fictional source input

Audience: mixed office staff. Time: 30 minutes. Focus: structure, tone, and a quick checklist before sending. Should include one short practice activity. Keep it practical.

Weak prompt

Weak promptCreate a training on emails.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • Extremely vague — "a training on emails" has no usable goal.
  • No audience, duration, or session focus from the source brief.
  • No required deliverables — learning objectives and timed outline are missing.
  • No mention of a practice activity or practical checklist focus.
  • No role, constraints or output format to keep the session to 30 minutes.

Your notes:

Your task

Write a prompt that produces three clear learning objectives and a timed 30-minute outline with one activity, suitable for mixed office staff.

Hint: specify the audience, duration, number of objectives and the required activity.

Case Study 8 · Capstone

ChatGPT-to-Gamma presentation

This is a fictional workshop scenario and not an official pharmaceutical record.

Scenario

You must prepare a fictional six-slide internal presentation introducing a simple "clear email" checklist to office teams, then generate it in Gamma. This capstone combines everything.

Fictional source input

Purpose: help office teams write clearer internal emails. Audience: mixed office staff. Duration of talk: about 8 minutes. Key ideas: state the purpose first, keep it short, use a clear subject, one ask per email, check before sending. Tone: friendly and practical.

Weak prompt

Weak promptMake me a presentation about emails in Gamma.

What is wrong with the weak prompt?

  • Too vague — topic only; no slide count, talk length or storyline.
  • No audience, purpose or tone from the source brief.
  • No source grounding — the checklist ideas are not attached as inputs.
  • No ChatGPT output format (per-slide title, message, points, visual).
  • No constraints against invented statistics or text-heavy slides before Gamma.

Your notes:

Your task

Write a ChatGPT prompt that produces a six-slide outline (title, one key message, up to three points, suggested visual per slide), review it, then note how you would generate and refine it in Gamma.

Framework reminder: see Resource 07 for the full ChatGPT-to-Gamma workflow and the reusable presentation prompt.
