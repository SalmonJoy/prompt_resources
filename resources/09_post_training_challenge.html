Resource 09 · Post-Training Challenge

One-Week Application Challenge

Practice is what turns a workshop into a skill. Over the next week, apply the advanced framework to one real but safe workplace task and document what you learn.

All resources

Instructions

Select one repetitive workplace task that is safe and approved for AI use. Use fictional or sanitised information where needed. Apply the framework, improve your prompt through follow-ups, and record your experience below.

Before you start
Do not use confidential, personal, proprietary, audit-sensitive, clinical, formula-related, regulatory or credential information in public AI tools without organisational approval. Company policy takes precedence.

Documentation template

1. Original requirement

What were you asked to produce, in its original form?

2. Business objective

What outcome does this task really need to achieve?

3. Initial prompt

The first prompt you wrote.

4. Summary of the first output

Briefly, what did ChatGPT produce?

5. Problems identified

What was weak, missing, inaccurate or off-tone?

6. Improved prompt

Your revised prompt using the framework and follow-ups.

7. Summary of the improved output

What changed and improved?

8. Human-review steps

What did you check or verify before using it?

9. Estimated time saved

A rough, honest estimate — avoid exaggeration.

10. Lessons learned
11. What you would change next time

Scoring rubric

Use this rubric to score your own submission out of 100%.

  • 20% Task framing
  • 20% Context quality
  • 15% Constraint design
  • 15% Output design
  • 15% Refinement process
  • 15% Validation and responsible use

Reflection

What did ChatGPT do well?
Where did it struggle?
Which instruction produced the biggest improvement?
What still required human expertise?
Remember
The goal is not to prove AI is perfect. It is to show that a well-framed, well-reviewed prompt produces a better, safer draft — and that the human remains accountable for the result.
