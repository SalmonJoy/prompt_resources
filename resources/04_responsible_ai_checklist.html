Responsible AI Checklist

A quick participant guide to using public AI tools responsibly at work. When in doubt, do not upload, and ask the relevant owner.

Generally safer examples

  • Fictional scenarios
  • Publicly available information
  • Generic templates
  • Anonymised notes
  • Content explicitly approved for AI use

Do not upload to public AI tools without approval

  • Patient or personal data
  • Clinical information
  • Product formulas
  • Confidential SOPs
  • Proprietary manufacturing information
  • Deviation or investigation records
  • Audit-sensitive documents
  • Credentials and passwords
  • Internal financial information
  • Unpublished R&D information
  • Regulator-facing documents

Before using AI-generated content

  • Verify facts
  • Verify references
  • Check calculations
  • Preserve uncertainty
  • Obtain subject-matter review
  • Follow approved company processes
  • Keep final accountability with the human user
