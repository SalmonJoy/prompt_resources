How to use this library
On screen, use the Copy Prompt button on any prompt, paste it into ChatGPT and replace every bracketed placeholder. The copy buttons are hidden when you print. All examples are fictional or generic.

Writing and Communication

1. Professional email

When to use You need a clear, polite email and want the right tone first time.

Reusable promptAct as [YOUR ROLE]. Write a professional email to [RECIPIENT]. Goal: [WHAT THE READER SHOULD KNOW OR DO]. Context: [ONE OR TWO LINES OF BACKGROUND]. Include: [POINT 1], [POINT 2], [POINT 3]. Tone: [FORMAL / NEUTRAL / FRIENDLY]. Keep it under [NUMBER] words. Do not invent dates, names or figures; mark anything unknown as "to be confirmed".
Variables to replace
[YOUR ROLE], [RECIPIENT], goal, context, points, tone, word limit.
Suggested follow-ups
"Add a one-line summary at the top." · "Make the closing warmer without adding promises."
Expected output structure
Subject line, greeting, short body, clear request, sign-off.

Human review: confirm names, dates and any commitment before sending.

2. Escalation email

When to use You must raise an issue upward calmly and factually.

Reusable promptAct as [YOUR ROLE]. Write a measured escalation email to [SENIOR RECIPIENT] about [ISSUE]. Include: what is happening, why it matters now, what has already been tried, and the specific decision or support needed. Tone: professional and non-blaming. Keep it under 180 words. State facts only; do not exaggerate impact or assign blame.
Variables to replace
[YOUR ROLE], [SENIOR RECIPIENT], [ISSUE].
Suggested follow-ups
"Add a short options paragraph with two possible next steps." · "Soften the urgency wording."
Expected output structure
Subject, one-line summary, issue, impact, action requested.

Human review: ensure the facts and impact are accurate and proportionate.

Thinking and Analysis

20. Option comparison

When to use Weighing two or more choices objectively.

Reusable promptCompare [OPTION A] and [OPTION B] for [GOAL] in a table. Rows: Cost, Time, Effort, Main benefit, Main risk. Keep each cell under 15 words. After the table, give a neutral one-line summary. Present this for human decision; do not choose for me.
Variables to replace
[OPTION A], [OPTION B], [GOAL].
Suggested follow-ups
"Add a third option." · "Add a 'best if...' line per option."
Expected output structure
Comparison table plus summary.

Human review: validate assumptions behind cost, time and risk.

21. Risk identification

When to use Surfacing risks before a decision or task.

Reusable promptI am planning [ACTIVITY]. Context: [BACKGROUND]. List up to [NUMBER] possible risks. For each: a one-line description, a likely impact, and a possible mitigation. Flag which risks depend on assumptions I should verify.
Variables to replace
[ACTIVITY], [BACKGROUND], [NUMBER].
Suggested follow-ups
"Rank by potential impact." · "Separate risks I control from those I do not."
Expected output structure
Risk / impact / mitigation list.

Human review: treat this as a prompt for thinking, not a risk assessment.

Responsible use
Use fictional or sanitised information when practising. Do not paste confidential, personal, proprietary, audit-sensitive, clinical, formula-related, regulatory or credential information into public AI tools without organisational approval. Every output is a draft until a human reviews it.
